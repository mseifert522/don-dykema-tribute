Remembering Don

Some people fill a room with noise. Don filled it with presence — steady, kind, and sure. You always knew where you stood with him, and you always knew you were loved.

On June 26, 2026, Donald C. Dykema of Nashville, Michigan, passed from this life into the arms of forever — leaving behind a family who will carry his laughter, his work ethic, and his quiet way of showing up for the people he loved.

His life

A Story Worth Telling

Don Beloved husband, father & grandfather
  • Born November 2, 1964 · Zeeland, Michigan
  • Raised Hudsonville, Michigan
  • Married May 22, 2010 · Dianne Louise Henry
  • Career 32 years · Bosch Fuel Systems, Kentwood
  • Home Nashville, Michigan

Born on November 2, 1964, in Zeeland, Michigan, Don spent his childhood in Hudsonville as the beloved son of Harold “Chet” and Arlene (Kennedy) Dykema. Those early years shaped the man he became — grounded, dependable, and deeply loyal to family.

On May 22, 2010, he married the second love of his life, Dianne Louise Henry. Together they built a life centered on family, hard work, and the kind of simple joys that don’t need a stage — only a backyard, a grill, and the people you love sitting close.

For thirty-two years, Don gave his best to Bosch Fuel Systems in Kentwood. Coworkers knew him as the one you could count on — respected for a strong work ethic and a dependability that never wavered. That same steadiness followed him home, onto the farm, and into every relationship he held dear.

Don will be remembered for his quiet strength, his generous heart, and his unwavering love for his family. He found happiness in life’s simplest pleasures — a day on the farm, a round of golf, a successful hunt, or a meal fresh off the grill shared with those he loved. His kindness, his steady presence, and his willingness to lend a helping hand touched more lives than he ever would have claimed.

Though he will be deeply missed, his love, his laughter, and the memories he created with family and friends will be cherished forever.

What made him smile

Fairways, friendships, and the easy rhythm of a game played more for the company than the scorecard.

A Successful Hunt

Patience in the woods, respect for the land, and the pride of a day spent doing what he loved outdoors.

Fresh Off the Grill

Smoke rising, plates filling, family gathering close. A meal was never just food — it was love, served hot.
